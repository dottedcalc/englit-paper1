Reasoning 0–4 — sub-scores (a), (b), (c)

Total reasoning = (a) + (b) + (c). The hover table lists each part when present; the justification quotes student wording for each.

  • (a) Deducible conclusion (0–2) — explains the evidence and shows how it proves the claim sentence by sentence; 2 = clear mechanism, 1 = minor leap or confusion, 0 = unclear or unjustified.
  • (b) Precise concept & wording (0–1) — concepts match what the evidence supports; no bait-and-switch, mid-argument shift, or broader claim than the evidence allows.
  • (c) Linear / not circular (0–1) — 1 = linear chain with clear purpose between sentences; 0 = circular, redundant, confusing, or assumes the conclusion.
